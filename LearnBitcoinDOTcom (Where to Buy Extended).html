Investing

While introducing Coinbase, I mentioned the word investor. While Bitcoin does not have a central company operating it, it does act like stock. The price is driven on supply and demand. With a limited supply of less than 21 million, the demand is the biggest factor for the price. As more people discover Bitcoin, they are having to buy at a higher price as the supply now has to be spread among the rest of the world population of 7 billion people. You are investing your money to use this service, but you are also investing on future gains whether you want to or not. This is because others also want this service. The number of bitcoins you have really does not matter in the Bitcoin system so when people buy, whether bitcoin is worth $1000 or $10,000 you can buy and send $10 worth. While the price is very volatile, or moves up and down a lot; it is going in a upward trend, however. As increasingly more people come into the system the supply will stay fixed but the demand will go up. The supply has not been entirely truthful, however. Most investors do not look in depth of what they are investing in. Many people follow the hype and know a basic amount about bitcoin, such as there are only going to be 21 million coins. What they probably do not know is that roughly 25% have been lost forever.
