Mobile Wallet

If you want to have a small amount of bitcoin, more than likely you want to spend it. The quickest, cheapest, and most convienent way is to store it on a mobile wallet. A small amount would be less than $100, BUT always remember to invest only what you can live without. The price of bitcoin is VERY volitile; this means value could drop unexpectedly and you would take a loss if you spend you BTC. If you want to invest more, you may want to read up on Bitcoin, as you should for any investment. Learn about other wallets and how Bitcoin actually works. This site is intended for casual users. If you wish to invest more, click the link below to one of the best hardware wallets on the market.

Whether you have an IPhone or an Android, you can go to the App store or Playstore and search for Bitcoin Wallet. You should find this app.


Once installed, you will see a screen similar to this, minus the transactions at first. Notice the wallet is in mBTC, not full BTC.



Units of Bitcoin

I mentioned that the wallet is in mBTC and not BTC. Units of bitcoin are not just measured in BTC, you can break them down to 0.00000001 BTC (that is one-one hundred millionth, or eight digits behind the decimal.) Its like a smaller change on the US Dollar. This is referred to as a Satoshi; after the alias of the creator of Bitcoin.

Mobile Wallet Safety Measures and Backing Up

Your phone may be stolen, and maybe the theif knows your phone unlock password or it was left unlocked. Most can also almost guarantee your phone isn't coming back. You can actually backup your wallet onto another device, and you should! A spending pin may be added on your wallet to prevent any fraud transaction.
When you have the wallet open on your phone, click on the icon in the top right corner.

Here we see the list of options we can select, click on the "safety" tab.

You should take some time and read the two options circled in red. It is just some knowlegde you should know as a wallet user. The green option will allow you to create a copy of your wallet.

You should select a strong password. One can be generated here: random password generator. One thing you may want to consider is get a small flashdrive that you can store off of your computer. You are the only one who knows the passwords so you do not want to forget/lose them. You should be able to send them to somewhere else other than your phone. I would advise you to email the file to yourself and save it on your computer or flashdrive, recommend on the flashdrive; just make sure you save it off of your phone.

If you ever need to restore your wallet on your phone, make sure the file is on your phone. If you did not back it up you are out of luck as you have ZERO ways to restore your wealth. Once you have it uploaded, select the top right "extra options" icon. You will now want to select "safety", then the "restore wallet" option.

If you have multiple wallet files you can select your most recent file with the green circled drop-down option. You may have different passwords for different wallets. Make sure you record which password is for which wallet.

The wallet is able to set up a pin that you must input before you are allowed to spend bitcoins, similar to the way a debit card works. You should also write this down in case you forget it, as you will have frozen your wallet with the lack of a pin.

Limited Supply

There are going to be a lot of numbers, and I apologize for that. Bitcoin, unfortunately for most people, runs on a lot of numbers. I am going to try and keep the numbers to a minimum. That being said, one important number is 21 million. 21 million is the rounded number for the total creation of bitcoins to ever be in existence. There will never be any more ever created, however, they can be destroyed, lost, or forgotten about. The actual number of bitcoins in supply or curculation is way less. With a growing population currently around 7 Billion people, 21 Million whole bitcoins would not work well for a global economy. By having smaller portions of a bitcoin, this feature allows small amounts to be traded to accurately pinpoint a value for the trade.
