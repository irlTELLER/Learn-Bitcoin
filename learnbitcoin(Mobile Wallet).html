Mobile Wallet

If you want to have a small amount of bitcoin, more than likely you want to spend it. The quickest, cheapest, and most convienent way is to store it on a mobile wallet. A small amount would be around $100, BUT always remember to invest only what you can live without. The price of bitcoin is VERY volitile; the price could drop unexpectedly and you would take a lose. If you want to invest more, you may want to read up on Bitcoin more, as you should for any investment, and learn about other wallets and how Bitcoin actually works. This is for casual users, however.

Whether you have an IPhone or an Android, you can go to the app store or Playstore and search for Bitcoin Wallet. You should find this app.


Once installed, you will see a screen similar to this, minus the transactions at first. Notice the wallet is in mBTC, not full BTC.



Units of Bitcoin

I mentioned that the wallet is in mBTC and not BTC. Units of bitcoin are not just measured in BTC, you can break them down to 0.00000001 BTC (that is one-one hundred millionth, or eight digits behind the decimal. Its like a smaller change on the US Dollar). This is refered to as a Satoshi, after the alias of the creator of Bitcoin.

Limited Supply

There are going to be a lot of numbers, and I apologize for that. Bitcoin, unfortunately for most people, runs on a lot of numbers. I am going to try and keep the numbers to a minimum. That being said, one important number is 21 million. 21 million is the rounded number for the total number of bitcoins to ever be in existence. There will never be any more ever created, but they can be destroyed, lost, or forgotten about. So the actual number of bitcoins in supply or curculation is way less. With a growing population currently around 7 Billion people, 21 Million whole bitcoins would not work well for a global economy. This feature allows small amounts to be traded to accurately pinpoint a value for the trade.
